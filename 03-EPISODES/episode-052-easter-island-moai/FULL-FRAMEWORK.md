### EXPERIMENTAL ARCHAEOLOGY = PROOF

**Thor Heyerdahl (1955-1956):**

**Carving Experiment:**

**Moving Experiment (Heyerdahl):**
- **Method:** Sledge + ropes (180 people pulled 12-ton moai, short distance)
- **Limitation:** Required many people, but WORKED

### THE "WALKING" METHOD (GAME CHANGER)

**Dr. Jo Anne Van Tilburg (UCLA, 1990s):**

**Theory:**
- **Moai walked upright** (not lying flat on sledges)
- **Method:** Roped around head/body, rocked side-to-side (like moving refrigerator)

**Experiment (1998):**
- **Team:** 18 people
- **Moai:** 10-ton replica
- **Result:** Moved hundreds of yards (slow, but WORKED)

**Oral Tradition:**
- **Rapa Nui legends:** "Moai walked to platforms" (literal truth, not myth)

### TERRY HUNT & CARL LIPO (2012 DEFINITIVE PROOF)

**Full-Scale Experiment:**
- **Moai:** 10-foot, multi-ton concrete replica
- **Team:** 18 people + 3 ropes
- **Method:** Rocked side-to-side, "walked" forward

**Video Evidence:**
- **Filmed:** Showed moai advancing (tilting, waddling)
- **Efficient:** Moved steadily with practice

**Published:**
- **National Geographic, scientific journals**
- **Consensus:** Mystery SOLVED

**Skeptic:**

**Core Samples (1980s-1990s):**

**Timeline:**
- **Early moai:** Trees available (used for rollers, levers, ramps)

**Skeptic:**
