### Cranial Deformation 101

Let's start with the basics: cranial deformation is real, well-documented, and thoroughly understood.

Cultures worldwide practiced it for thousands of years:
- **Mayans and Aztecs** (Mexico/Central America)
- **Ancient Egyptians** (especially New Kingdom elite)
- **Pacific Islanders** (Vanuatu, Hawaii)
- **African tribes** (Mangbetu people)
- **European tribes** (Huns, Sarmatians)
- **Paracas and Nazca cultures** (Peru)

The practice involved wrapping an infant's soft skull with cloth, boards, or bindings to reshape it as the child grew. The skull bones are malleable for the first few years of life, allowing dramatic reshaping.

This is not controversial. This is established fact, documented in mummies, artwork, and historical accounts.

### The Paracas Skulls Are Textbook Deformation

Now let's look specifically at the Paracas skulls.

- **Pressure marks** where bindings compressed the skull

The elongation follows predictable patterns based on binding methodology:
- **Annular deformation** (circumferential binding) produces a conical, elongated shape

The Paracas skulls show annular modificationâ€”exactly what you'd expect from the Paracas culture's documented practices.

### The Volume Issue Is a Myth

Let's address the "50% larger" claim. It's misleading.

**Fact 1:** The normal human cranial capacity range is 1,000-2,000 cubic centimeters. The average is 1,350, but there's enormous natural variation.

**Fact 2:** Cranial binding *can* slightly increase total volume by forcing the skull to expand in the allowed direction. If you compress front-to-back, the skull compensates by expanding upward or laterally.

**Fact 3:** The Paracas skulls fall within the upper range of normal human variation. They're not outliers. They're not alien. They're large humans with reshaped skulls.

Brien Foerster cherry-picks the largest examples and compares them to average skulls, creating the illusion of dramatic difference.

Physical anthropologists who examined the skulls found typical human cranial sutures, including the sagittal suture separating the two parietal bones. What Foerster interpreted as "one plate" was likely:
- Misidentification of the lambdoid or coronal suture
- Damage or erosion obscuring suture lines
- Misunderstanding of cranial anatomy

No accredited anthropologist has confirmed this claim. It's amateur analysis by someone without the training to interpret cranial morphology.

### The DNA "Findings" Are Nonsense

Let's talk about those "anomalous" DNA results.

Foerster provided fragments of results, verbal descriptions, and vague references to "mutations." No peer-reviewed paper. No detailed genetic analysis. No raw data.

**Problem 2: Ancient DNA is commonly degraded.**

DNA from 2,000-year-old remains is often contaminated, fragmentary, and difficult to analyze. Partial sequences, bacterial DNA, and degradation artifacts are common.

When Foerster says results were "unclassifiable," that likely means the DNA was too degraded to get clean readsâ€”not that it was alien.

If the Paracas skulls were alien or hybrid, genetic studies would show it. They don't.
