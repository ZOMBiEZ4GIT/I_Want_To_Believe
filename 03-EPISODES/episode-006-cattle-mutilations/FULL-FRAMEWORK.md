### THE EVIDENCE

**Characteristics Reported:**

1. **Precision "Cuts":**
   - Straight-line incisions
   - Removal of specific organs (always the same ones)

   - Believers claim: "surgical precision"

2. **No Blood:**

## ðŸ”¬ SKEPTIC'S RESPONSE (22 min)

### NATURAL EXPLANATIONS

**Predator/Scavenger Behavior:**

**What Actually Happens When Animals Decompose:**

1. **Soft Tissue Goes First:**
   - Eyes, tongue, anus, udders are **soft and accessible**
   - Scavengers (coyotes, foxes, birds) eat these FIRST
   - This is normal scavenging behavior worldwide

2. **Bloating Creates "Clean" Tears:**
   - Decomposition gases build up
   - Skin stretches, becomes thin
   - When scavengers tear at bloated skin, edges appear smooth
   - **Not surgicalâ€”just bloated skin tearing cleanly**

3. **Blood Pools Internally:**
   - Gravity pulls blood to lowest points after death
   - Blood coagulates, looks "drained"
   - Scavengers eat blood-soaked organs, leaving dry carcass

4. **Blowflies and Larvae:**
   - Flies target soft, moist tissues (eyes, mouth, orifices)
   - Larvae consume tissue rapidly
   - Creates appearance of "removed" organs

**FBI Investigation (1970s) - "Operation Animal Mutilation":**
- **Investigator:** Kenneth Rommel, recently retired FBI agent
  - **Background:** 28-year FBI veteran specializing in counter-intelligence and major crimes (bank robbery, etc.)
- **Timeline:** Investigation began May 1979
- **Funding:** $44,170 grant from Law Enforcement Assistance Administration (LEAA)
- **Objectives:**
  1. Determine reliability of mutilation reports
  2. Determine cause of as many mutilations as possible
  3. Assess if mutilations constitute major law enforcement problem
  4. Provide recommendations or recommend no further investigations
- **Final Report (Released June 1980):**
  - **Length:** 297 pages
  - **Estimate:** Acknowledged "by 1979, 10,000 head of cattle have been mysteriously mutilated"
  - **Conclusion:** Mutilations predominantly result of **natural predation**, though some cases contained anomalies that couldn't be accounted for by conventional wisdom
  - **Key Finding:** FBI unable to identify any individuals responsible for mutilations
  - No evidence of human involvement
  - No evidence of cult activity (despite satanic panic theories)
  - **Report:** Consistent with scavenger activity
- **Criticism:**
  - Canadian RCMP investigator **Corporal Lyn Lauber** criticized Rommel's conclusions
  - Lauber: "I find it difficult to understand how Rommel could make a statement such as this, **without ever having personally witnessed a [real] mutilation firsthand**"
- **Availability:** Investigation details now available under Freedom of Information Act

**Colorado Study (1975):**
- State-funded investigation during peak mutilation wave
- Colorado Bureau of Investigation led by Carl Whiteside
- Veterinary pathologists examined carcasses at Colorado State University
- **19 necropsies performed** on allegedly mutilated animals
- **Conclusion:** Predators and normal decomposition
- **Problem:** Ranchers rejected findings (too invested in mystery)

**Washington County Sheriff's Department Controlled Experiment:**
- **Setup:** Euthanized heifer placed in ravine and **watched 24 hours a day, day and night**
- **Timeline:** Officers recovered carcass after 30 hours
- **Results:**
  - Carcass showed **exact conditions reported in mutilation cases**
  - **Proof:** Only scavengers touched the cow (no human or alien intervention)
  - **Blowflies:** Laid eggs in soft tissues (eyes, ears, anus) within hours
  - **Maggots:** Created wounds that **looked like surgical cuts**
  - **Bloating:** In 48-hour observation, post-mortem bloating caused incision-like tears in skin that matched "surgical" cuts reported on mutilated cows
  - Blowfly and maggot action matched soft tissue damage in alleged mutilations
- **Significance:** Reproduced "mutilation" conditions through natural processes alone

**Peer-Reviewed Scientific Studies:**
- **Alberta Study (Canada):** Peer-reviewed research concluded cattle "mutilation" due to scavenging of carcasses
- Study stated: Claims of human involvement require **first excluding post-mortem scavenging**
- Veterinary pathologists who performed necropsies concluded cause of death was natural

### DEBUNKING SPECIFIC CLAIMS

**"Surgical Precision":**
- **Reality:** Bloated, stretched skin tears cleanly
- Scavengers have sharp teeth, create clean-looking wounds
- **Forensic test:** When examined by actual forensic veterinarians, "cuts" show tooth marks and tearing
- **Washington County Experiment Proof:**
  - Natural bloating in 48 hours created incision-like tears identical to "surgical" cuts
  - Blowflies and maggots consumed soft tissue, creating appearance of "removed" organs
  - Zero human or alien intervention required
- **Coyote Behavior:** In known coyote predations, coyotes bite and pull back, leaving what appears to be straight-line cut; small bite marks visible upon close examination
- **Scavenger Targeting:** Foxes, coyotes, and vultures target easiest meat on dead cowsâ€”the softest parts (tongue, anus, eyes)

**"No Blood":**
- **Reality:** Blood coagulates and pools internally
- Scavengers consume blood-soaked organs
- Dry appearance is POST-scavenging

**"No Tracks":**
- **Reality:** Carcasses often found days after death
- Scavengers are lightweight (don't leave obvious tracks)
- Wind, weather erase tracks in days
- Investigator bias: Looking for "tracks to UFO," ignoring natural disturbances

**"Scavenger Avoidance":**
- **Reality:** This is folklore, not documented behavior
- Some carcasses avoided initially due to disease/toxins (natural)
- Most carcasses ARE scavenged (just after soft tissues already gone)

**"Precision Always Same Organs":**
- **Reality:** These are the EASIEST organs for scavengers to access
- Of course scavengers take eyes, tongue, soft reproductive organs first
- This is what scavengers do everywhere on Earth
