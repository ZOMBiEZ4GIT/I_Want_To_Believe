## ðŸ‘½ BELIEVER'S CASE (25 min)

### THE WITNESSES

**Charles Hickson (1931-2011):**
- **Born:** April 16, 1931, Jones County, Mississippi
- Age 42 at time of incident
- **Career:** Foreman at Walker Shipyard in Pascagoula (built U.S. Navy ships)
- Married to Blanche (died 2004 from rheumatoid arthritis), father
- **Korean War veteran:** Awarded five battle stars for heroic acts
- **Reputation:** Hardworking, honest, religious
- No history of UFO interest
- **Post-incident:** Gave hundreds of interviews, wrote book "UFO Contact at Pascagoula" (1983), never changed story
- Took multiple polygraph tests (passed)
- **Death:** September 9, 2011, age 80, heart attack in Ocean Springs; buried in McGill Cemetery, Sandersville

**Calvin Parker (1954-2023):**
- Age 19 at time of incident
- Shipyard worker (Hickson's co-worker)
- Shy, introverted
- **Reported:** Fainted from fear during abduction
- **Post-incident PTSD:** Emotionally distant, trouble sleeping, cold sweats, required medical help for nerves, developed bitterness toward media
- Avoided publicity for decades
- **2018 Book:** "Pascagoula - The Closest Encounter: My Story" (Flying Disk Press, foreword by Philip Mantle) - first full account given by Parker
- Maintained story happened exactly as described until death
- **Death:** August 24, 2023, age 68, after long battle with kidney cancer - just months before 50th anniversary of incident

**Key Detail:** These were not UFO enthusiasts. Hickson was skeptical of UFOs before the incident. Parker wanted nothing to do with the attention.

### THE TIMELINE

**October 11, 1973 - Evening**

**7:00 PM:**
- Hickson and Parker finish work shift at Walker Shipyard
- Decide to go fishing at abandoned Shaupeter Shipyard pier
- Location: Pascagoula River, isolated area

**~8:45 PM - THE ENCOUNTER BEGINS:**

**The Craft:**
- Hickson and Parker fishing quietly
- Hear **buzzing/whirring sound** (like electric motor)
- Turn to see **blue flashing lights**
- **Craft appears:** Oval/football-shaped, metallic, 8-10 feet high, 30-40 feet across
- Hovers ~2 feet above ground, ~30 feet away
- **No landing gear visible** (floating)

**The Beings:**
- Door/opening appears in craft
- Three beings emerge, **floating** (not walking)
- Description:
  - **Height:** ~5 feet tall
  - **Skin:** Grey, wrinkled (like elephant skin)
  - **Head:** Dome-shaped, no visible eyes or nose
  - **Mouth:** Small slit (no movement/speech)
  - **Hands:** Claw-like appendages (mitten-shaped, no individual fingers)
  - **Legs:** Fused or robotic (didn't bend at knees)
  - **Movement:** Glided/floated above ground
  - **Sound:** Buzzing noise when moving

**~8:50 PM - THE ABDUCTION:**

**Hickson's Experience:**
- Two beings approach him
- He tries to resistâ€”**cannot move** (paralyzed)
- Beings grab him under arms with claws
- Lifted off ground, floated toward craft
- **Conscious but immobilized**
- Quote: "I couldn't move. I was scared to death."

**Parker's Experience:**
- One being approaches him
- **Faints immediately** (claims he blacked out from fear)
- Wakes up inside craft (minimal memory of interior)

**Inside the Craft:**

**Hickson's Account (More Detailed):**
- Interior: Bright, diffuse light (no visible source)
- **Floating examination:** Levitated horizontally in air
- Large **eye-like device** scans him (moves over body)
- No verbal communication from beings
- Duration: ~15-20 minutes
- **Returned to pier** (floated back out)

**Parker's Account:**
- Fragmented memories (claims fear caused partial amnesia)
- Bright lights
- Sense of being examined
- Confirms beings present, examination occurred

**~9:10 PM - THE RETURN:**

- Both men deposited back on pier
- Craft departs (buzzing sound, blue lights disappear)
- Men left trembling, disoriented
- **First reaction:** "Did that just happen?"

**9:30 PM - THE DECISION TO REPORT:**

- Hickson and Parker discuss what to do
- Consider staying silent (fear of ridicule)
- **Hickson insists:** "We have to tell someone"
- Drive to Keesler Air Force Base (closed, can't report)
- Call Jackson County Sheriff's Office
- Drive to sheriff's office in Pascagoula

**10:30 PM - POLICE REPORT:**

- Sheriff Fred Diamond and Captain Glenn Ryder interview them
- **Initial reaction:** Skeptical (assume drunk or prank)
- Both men sober, genuinely terrified
- **No smell of alcohol, coherent stories**
- Police decide to test them with **secret recording**

### THE SECRET RECORDING (The Smoking Gun)

**The Setup:**
- **After 30 minutes of questioning:** Sheriff Fred Diamond decided to test them
- Police leave Hickson and Parker alone in interrogation room
- **Hidden microphone recording** (men don't know)
- Purpose: Catch them laughing, admitting hoax
- **Tape held on file at Jackson County Sheriff's department since 1973**

**What Was Recorded:**

**Hickson:** "I'll tell you one thing. I ain't crazy."
**Parker:** (Crying, hyperventilating) "I know we ain't. I know we ain't crazy."
**Hickson:** "I know we're not. But what's hard to believe is what we saw."
**Parker:** "I couldn't move, Charlie. I couldn't move. I was scared to death."
**Hickson:** "I was too, son. I was too."

**Parker:** (Repeatedly) "I want to see a doctor."

**Later:**
**Hickson:** "Jesus Christ, have mercy on me. I know there's a God up there. I know there's a God up there."

**Police Reaction:**
- Sheriff Diamond assumed if lying, it would become immediately apparent when speaking privately
- Instead: Men remained highly distressed, never strayed from fantastic tale
- Listened to recording
- **Conclusion:** "These men are genuinely terrified. They believe what they're saying."
- Released without charges
- **No evidence of hoax**

**Why This Matters:**
- If hoax, they would've relaxed when alone
- Instead: Praying, crying, comforting each other, Parker requesting medical help
- **Genuine fear captured on tape**
- Recording became key evidence supporting men's believability

### THE INVESTIGATION

**Dr. J. Allen Hynek (Astrophysicist, former Project Blue Book consultant):**
- **Arrived in Pascagoula within 36 hours of incident**
- Interviewed Hickson and Parker separately
- **Some interviews conducted under hypnosis**
- **Conclusion:** "These men have had a very real, frightening experience. They are absolutely honest."
- Called case "deeply puzzling" - "one of the most compelling I have encountered in my research"
- **Published detailed account in "The UFO Experience" (1974)**
- **Recent discovery:** Audio files of Hynek's interviews literally within hours of reported abduction have been found

**Polygraph Tests:**
- Hickson: Tested multiple times (passed)
- Parker: Tested (passed)
- Operators concluded: **Both believe their story is true**

**Hypnotic Regression (Hickson):**
- Dr. James Harder (UC Berkeley) conducted session
- Hickson relived encounter under hypnosis
- **Consistent with conscious memory**
- Showed extreme emotional distress

**Media Circus:**
- Story went national (front-page news)
- Hickson gave interviews (reluctantly)
- Parker **avoided media** (traumatized)

**Other Witnesses:**
- **Larry Booth & wife:** Reported seeing blue light in sky same night, same area
- **Coast Guard helicopter pilot:** Reported unusual lights

### ALTERNATIVE EXPLANATIONS

**Explanation 1: SHARED HALLUCINATION (Folie Ã  Deux)**

**What It Is:**
- **Folie Ã  deux:** Shared psychotic disorder
- One person (primary) has delusion, influences another (secondary)
- Secondary adopts primary's delusion as reality

**How It Could Apply:**
- Hickson (older, authority figure) had hallucination
- Shared experience (fishing at night, tired, maybe stressed)
- Parker (younger, impressionable) influenced by Hickson's fear
- **Both genuinely believe** (explains polygraph, secret recording)

**Problems With This Theory:**
- Folie Ã  deux rare, usually requires extended contact (they were co-workers but not isolated)
- Hallucinations don't usually match this precisely between two people
- **Doesn't explain other witnesses (lights seen by others)**

**Explanation 2: HOAX FOR ATTENTION/MONEY**

**The Theory:**
- Elaborate prank
- Secret recording was act (they knew about microphone)
- Goal: Fame, book deals, speaking fees

**Evidence Against:**
- **Parker avoided publicity** for 45 years (no book until 2018)
- Hickson gave interviews but **donated earnings** to charity
- Both resisted media initially
- **No smoking gun admission** (even after Hickson's death, no deathbed confession)

**Skeptic Counterpoint:**
"Maybe they committed to the bit so hard they started believing it."

**Believers Respond:**
"That's not how psychology works. They'd show some crack in 50 years."

**Explanation 3: SLEEP PARALYSIS-LIKE EVENT**

**The Theory:**
- Hickson experienced hypnagogic/hypnopompic hallucination (waking dream)
- Fell asleep while fishing (late evening, tired after work shift)
- Dream felt real (vivid, terrifying)
- Woke Parker, shared fear, induced panic

**How Sleep Paralysis Explains Some Details:**
- **Paralysis:** Sleep paralysis creates sensation of being unable to move
- **Beings:** Common in sleep paralysis (shadowy figures, aliens)
- **Floating:** Out-of-body sensations in sleep paralysis

**Problems:**
- Both men reported paralysis simultaneously (unlikely)
- Parker's independent experience (doesn't match sleep paralysis model)
- **Too coherent** (sleep paralysis usually fragmented)

**Explanation 4: MISIDENTIFICATION + PANIC**

**The Theory:**
- Saw something mundane (helicopter, swamp gas, experimental aircraft)
- Poor lighting + fear = misidentified as UFO
- **Panic escalated** (brain filled in alien details)

**What They Might Have Seen:**
- Military helicopter (Keesler AFB nearby)
- Bioluminescent phenomenon (river location)
- Distant lights from shipyard/industrial area

**Beings:**
- Could've been workers in protective suits (welding gear, hazmat)
- Darkness + fear = "robot aliens"

**Problems:**
- Very detailed descriptions (hard to mistake people in suits)
- Physical paralysis reported (not just fear)
- **Other witnesses** saw unusual lights (not helicopters)

### PROBLEMS WITH THE CASE

**Issue 1: NO PHYSICAL EVIDENCE**

**What's Missing:**
- No photographs of craft or beings
- No soil samples from landing site
- No radiation readings
- **No physical trace of 30-40 foot craft hovering**

**Skeptic Point:**
"Extraordinary claims require extraordinary evidence. All we have is testimony."

**Issue 2: PARKER'S MEMORY GAPS**

**Skeptic Concern:**
- Parker claims he fainted, has fragmented memory
- **Convenient amnesia?**
- Allows vague corroboration without contradicting Hickson

**Believer Response:**
"Trauma causes memory gaps. Parker was terrifiedâ€”fainting is understandable."

**Issue 3: POLYGRAPH TESTS ARE PSEUDOSCIENCE**

**Skeptic Reality Check:**
- Polygraphs measure stress, not truth
- **Not admissible in most courts** (unreliable)
- Passing polygraph means: "They believe it," not "It's true"
- People can pass polygraphs while lying (psychopaths, true believers)

**What It Actually Proves:**
- Hickson and Parker **believed** their story
- **Doesn't prove aliens**

**Issue 4: ONLY TWO PRIMARY WITNESSES**

**The Problem:**
- All details come from Hickson and Parker
- Other witnesses saw "lights" (vague, could be anything)
- **No independent verification of beings, craft details**

**Skeptic Standard:**
"Two witnesses to extraordinary event = not enough. Need physical evidence."

**Issue 5: 1973 WAS "YEAR OF THE UFO"**

**Cultural Context:**
- **October 1973:** Massive wave of UFO sightings across U.S.
- Media saturation (UFO stories everywhere)
- **Hickson and Parker exposed to UFO narratives before their incident**
- Possible unconscious influence on their experience/description

**Skeptic Interpretation:**
"They saw something, panicked, brain filled in details from recent UFO stories."

### PSYCHOLOGICAL FACTORS

**The Power of Belief:**
- Once they reported, **impossible to back down**
- Social pressure (family, community, media)
- **Reputations on the line**
- Admitting hoax/mistake = humiliation

**Memory Reconstruction:**
- Every retelling solidifies memory
- After 50 years, they remember the STORY, not necessarily the EVENT
- **Details added unconsciously** over time

**Trauma Bonding:**
- Shared experience created bond between Hickson & Parker
- **Reinforced each other's belief**
- Impossible to admit doubt without betraying the other
