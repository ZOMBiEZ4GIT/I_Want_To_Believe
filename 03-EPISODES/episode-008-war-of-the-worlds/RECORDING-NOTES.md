# RECORDING NOTES: Episode 008 - War of the Worlds

## Pre-Recording Prep
- **Key dramatic beats to emphasize:**
  - October 30, 1938: You're listening to music, bulletin interrupts - Martians have landed!
  - The brilliant fake news bulletin format that made it feel SO real
  - Next day's newspaper headlines screaming about "mass panic"
  - The truth: newspapers LIED to attack their competitor (radio)
  - Modern research shows maybe 1,000-2,000 actually panicked out of 6 million listeners

- **Voices needed:**
  - Orson Welles: Young, dramatic, theatrical (he was 23!)
  - 1938 radio announcer: Urgent news bulletin voice
  - Newspaper headlines: Sensational, accusatory
  - Modern historians: Matter-of-fact correcting the record

- **Complex sections to mark:**
  - Why newspapers had motive to lie (radio stealing ad revenue)
  - The actual listenership numbers and panic estimates
  - How most listeners understood it was fiction
  - The Princeton study showing panic was ABOUT the panic, not the broadcast
  - Why this matters for UFO culture (media shapes belief)

- **Rant opportunities:**
  - Traditional: "Welles was SO convincing America thought we were being invaded!"
  - Skeptical correction: "The panic was exaggerated! Newspapers LIED to discredit radio!"
  - "If War of the Worlds panic is myth, what else is?"
  - "Same pattern as UFOs: small event → media amplification → legend"

## Timing Targets
