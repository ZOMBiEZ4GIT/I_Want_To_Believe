## ðŸ“– OVERVIEW

The Exeter Incident refers to sightings of a large object with pulsating red lights witnessed by multiple people in Exeter, New Hampshire on September 3, 1965. Primary witnesses were teenager Norman Muscarello and police officers Eugene Bertrand and David Hunt. The object was described as silent, massive (60-90 feet), with five brilliant red lights flashing sequentially. Project Blue Book investigated, initially attributing it to military exercises, then later listing it as "unidentified." The case gained national attention through John G. Fuller's book "Incident at Exeter" (1966).

**THE BELIEVER'S CLAIM:** The Exeter Incident is one of the strongest UFO cases due to multiple credible witnesses (including two police officers), consistent descriptions, official investigation, and lack of conventional explanation. The object's size, silent hovering, pulsating lights, and maneuvers rule out aircraft, satellites, or natural phenomena. The witnesses' sincerity and the object's physical effects (illuminating field, frightening animals) indicate genuine anomalous craft. Project Blue Book's inability to explain it validates its significance.

**THE SKEPTICAL RESPONSE:** The object was likely aircraft from Pease Air Force Base conducting night operations, possibly refueling exercises with bright landing lights. The witnesses misjudged distance and size in darkness. The "pulsating" effect was misinterpreted rotating beacon or multiple aircraft lights. The Air Force's initial "Operation Big Blast" explanation was partially correct (military activity in area). Mass hysteria and media amplification created wave of copycat sightings. The case is explainable by conventional aircraft misidentification.

**THE REALITY:** The Exeter Incident remains genuinely puzzling. While aircraft explanations are plausible, they don't fully satisfy all witness descriptions. The case represents high-quality UFO evidence with credible multiple witnesses, but lacks physical proof beyond testimony.

### The Witnesses Misjudged Distance and Altitude

**Estimating size and distance of lights at night is extremely difficult:**

**Example:** Hold a flashlight at arm's length in the dark. Ask someone to estimate its distance. Most will underestimate by factor of 3-5.

**If actual:**
- Altitude: 5,000 feet
- Size: 100 feet (large aircraft)

**Witnesses perceived:**

**1. Rotating beacon:** Aircraft have rotating red beacons

**3. Formation flying:** Multiple aircraft with staggered lights create sequential pattern

**The effect witnesses interpreted as "alien technology" is actually standard aircraft lighting.**
