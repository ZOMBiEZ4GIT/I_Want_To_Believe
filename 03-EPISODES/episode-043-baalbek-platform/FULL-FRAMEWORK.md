### HOW IT WAS DONE (SKEPTIC EXPLANATION)

**Dr. Jean-Pierre Adam (French Archaeologist/Engineer):**

**Reconstruction (1977 Study):**

**1. QUARRYING:**
- **Method:** Wooden wedges soaked in water (expand, crack stone)
- **Cutting:** Copper/bronze chisels + sand abrasive (time-consuming, but effective)
- **Duration:** Months per stone (not quick, but doable)

**2. MOVING:**
- **Wooden sledge:** Stone placed on platform
- **Rollers:** Wooden logs under sledge (reduce friction)
- **Grease:** Animal fat on rollers (lubrication)
- **Manpower:** ~500-1,000 workers per stone (not 40,000â€”believer calculation wrong)

**Physics:**
- **Friction coefficient:** Wood on wood + grease = ~0.1-0.2
- **Force needed:** ~80-160 tons of pulling force (distributed among workers = ~200-400 lbs per person)
- **Feasible:** With organization, achievable

**3. RAMPS:**
- **Earth ramps:** Built up to placement height
- **Temporary:** Removed after construction
- **Evidence:** Ramp remnants found at other Roman sites

**4. LIFTING/PLACEMENT:**
- **Lever systems:** Wood beams as levers (mechanical advantage)
- **Incremental lifting:** Raise stone bit by bit, add support logs, repeat
- **Precision:** Trial and error + skill (Roman engineers were experts)

**Skeptic Conclusion:**
"Slow, labor-intensive, but POSSIBLE. No aliens needed."

### EXPERIMENTS PROVE IT WORKS

**Modern Replications:**

**1990s Experiments (Various):**
