## THE LEARNING PHASE: WEEKS 1-4

You're not recording 100 episodes. You're learning a skill.

**Week 1:** Equipment and basics
**Week 2:** Your first episode
**Week 3:** Your second episode (better than the first)
**Week 4:** Establish your rhythm

## PRODUCTION MODE: WEEKS 5+

### The Weekly Rhythm

**You're not recording 100 episodes. You're recording 1-2 episodes per week for a year.**

That's sustainable. That's achievable. That's how you finish.

### Sample Weekly Schedule

**MONDAY - Planning & Prep (1 hour)**
- Pick next episode
- Read brief and recording notes
- Mark up script
- Set weekly goals

**TUESDAY - Research Day (Optional)**
- If needed, dive into full framework
- Make notes for difficult sections
- Practice voices/pronunciations

**WEDNESDAY - Recording Day (2-4 hours)**
- Morning: Best for high-energy cold opens
- Record full episode OR
- Record believer + skeptic sections (finish Thursday)

**THURSDAY - Recording or Editing (2-3 hours)**
- Finish recording if not done, OR
- Edit Wednesday's recording
- Remove mistakes, adjust pacing

**FRIDAY - Polish & Publish (1-2 hours)**
- Final edits
- Add music
- Normalize audio
- Export
- Update trackers
- CELEBRATE

**WEEKEND - Rest or Batch Prep**
- Option 1: Complete rest (recommended every other week)
- Option 2: Batch prep 2-3 episode briefs for upcoming weeks
- Option 3: Record a second episode if you're feeling it

**Weekly XP:** 275-550 XP depending on 1-2 episodes

### Brain-State-Based Task Selection

Your brain doesn't have the same energy every day. Match tasks to your mental state.

**HIGH ENERGY / HYPERFOCUS DAYS:**
- Record believer section (passion and drama)
- Record cold opens (need peak performance)
- Record full episode in one session (if you're in flow)
- Tackle difficult/complex episodes

**MEDIUM ENERGY DAYS:**
- Edit recordings
- Record skeptic section (analytical work)
- Record your take section (reflective)
- Prep upcoming episodes

**LOW ENERGY / SCATTERED DAYS:**
- Read episode briefs
- Update completion trackers
- Organize files
- Mark up recording notes
- Practice pronunciations
- Watch tutorials

**ZERO FOCUS / ROUGH DAYS:**
- Do NOTHING podcast-related
- OR do 5-minute tasks from `06-QUICK-WINS/5-minute-tasks.md`
- Forgiveness is built into the system
- Missing a day doesn't ruin momentum

### Batching vs. Focus

**Batching** (doing multiple similar tasks together):
- ‚úÖ Read 3-5 episode briefs in one session
- ‚úÖ Edit 2-3 episodes back-to-back
- ‚úÖ Record all cold opens for the week
- ‚ùå Record 3 full episodes in a day (burnout risk)

**Single-Focus** (one episode start to finish):
- ‚úÖ Deep engagement with material
- ‚úÖ Better narrative flow
- ‚úÖ More satisfying completion
- ‚ùå Can feel slow if you're in hyperfocus mode

**Experiment and find what works for YOU.**

## TROUBLESHOOTING ADHD CHALLENGES

### "I Can't Start"

**Symptoms:**
- Staring at episode list
- Reading briefs but not recording
- "Researching equipment" for the 10th time
- Everything feels too big

**Solutions:**

**5-Minute Rule:** Commit to just 5 minutes. Set a timer. Read ONE episode brief. Often, starting creates momentum.

**Reduce Friction:**
- Leave recording setup ready to go
- Have your marked-up script open
- Queue up the episode framework
- Remove ALL barriers between you and "record"

**Pick the Easiest Task:**
- Don't record - just read a brief
- Don't record a full episode - just record the cold open
- Don't record - just practice one difficult pronunciation

**Accountability:**
- Tell someone you're recording today
- Post on social media "Starting episode X"
- Join a podcasting community

**Remember:** Starting is the hardest part. Once you press record, flow often follows.

### "I'm Overwhelmed"

**Symptoms:**
- Looking at "100 episodes" and freezing
- Too many choices (which episode?)
- Too much information
- Feeling paralyzed

**Solutions:**

**Narrow Your Focus:**
- You're not recording 100 episodes today
- You're recording ONE episode
- Actually, you're just recording the cold open
- Actually, you're just reading the brief

**Use the Quick-Wins List:**
- Go to `06-QUICK-WINS/5-minute-tasks.md`
- Do ONE thing
- Check it off
- Dopamine hit received

**Close Unnecessary Tabs:**
- Open ONLY: episode brief, recording notes, recording software
- Close everything else
- Reduce cognitive load

**Reset:**
- Close your computer
- Walk away for 10 minutes
- Come back and do just ONE task

### "I've Lost Motivation"

**Symptoms:**
- Haven't touched podcast in days/weeks
- Guilt about not working
- Interest has faded
- "What's the point?"

**Solutions:**

**Revisit Your Why:**
- Why did you start this?
- Who is this for?
- What does completing this mean to you?
- Write it down

**Review Your Progress:**
- Look at completed episodes
- Listen to your early vs. recent work
- See how far you've come
- Celebrate the wins

**Lower the Bar:**
- Don't aim for 2 episodes/week
- Aim for 1 episode this month
- Or just prep one episode
- Or just read one brief
- Something > nothing

**Change Your Approach:**
- Record a different episode
- Try a different time of day
- Record in a different location
- Invite a friend to co-record

**Take a Break:**
- Seriously, step away for a week
- Guilt-free rest
- Come back fresh
- The episodes will wait

### "I Started But Can't Finish"

**Symptoms:**
- 47 half-recorded episodes
- Excitement for starting, dread for finishing
- Editing feels impossible
- "I'll finish it later" (you won't)

**Solutions:**

**One Episode at a Time:**
- Don't start a new episode until current is PUBLISHED
- Finishing > starting
- Build the "completion" muscle

**Finish-First Fridays:**
- Every Friday = finish something day
- Pick ONE half-done episode
- Finish and publish it
- No starting new things

**Reduce Scope:**
- "Done" = rough edit + export
- Don't aim for NPR-quality production
- Good enough is GOOD ENOUGH
- You can always remaster later

**Accountability Partner:**
- Tell someone "I'm finishing episode X this week"
- Check in daily
- Report completion

### "Perfectionism Paralysis"

**Symptoms:**
- Re-recording the same section 47 times
- Editing for hours on tiny details
- Never satisfied
- Not publishing because "not good enough"

**Solutions:**

**Set Time Limits:**
- Recording: Max 4 hours per episode
- Editing: Max 2 hours per episode
- When timer goes off, you're done

**The 80/20 Rule:**
- 80% of quality comes from 20% of effort
- First edit = 80% there
- Subsequent edits = diminishing returns
- STOP at "good enough"

**Version 1.0 Mindset:**
- This is V1.0
- You can always re-record later
- Done > perfect
- Your first 10 episodes will be rough - accept it

**Compare to Your Past, Not Others:**
- Don't compare to NPR/Radiolab
- Compare to YOUR episode 1
- Progress > perfection

### "I Keep Getting Distracted"

**Symptoms:**
- Start recording, end up on Reddit
- Research tangents lasting hours
- "Just checking" email/social
- Can't focus for full sessions

**Solutions:**

**Pomodoro Technique:**
- 25 minutes recording
- 5 minutes break
- Repeat
- Longer break after 4 rounds

**Eliminate Distractions:**
- Phone in another room
- Browser extensions blocking social media
- "Do Not Disturb" mode
- Headphones in (even if not playing music)

**Body Doubling:**
- Record while someone else works nearby
- Virtual co-working sessions
- Livestream yourself recording (accountability)

**Movement Breaks:**
- ADHD brains need movement
- Quick walk between sections
- Stretch, jump, dance
- Return refreshed

**Stimulation Management:**
- Some people need background noise
- Some need silence
- Experiment: music? White noise? Nothing?
- Find what works for YOUR brain

## EMERGENCY PROTOCOLS

### When Everything Feels Impossible

**STOP.**

Take a breath.

**You have options:**

**Option 1: Micro-Task**
- Do just ONE 5-minute task
- Read one episode brief
- Update one tracker
- That's enough for today

**Option 2: Complete Rest**
- Take a week off
- No guilt
- No podcast work
- Rest and reset

**Option 3: Change Something**
- Different episode
- Different approach
- Different time of day
- Different environment

**Option 4: Get Help**
- Ask for accountability
- Join a community
- Hire an editor (if budget allows)
- Find a co-host

### When You Want to Quit

**You Will Want to Quit.**

Around episode 15. Around episode 40. Around episode 75.

**That's normal.**

**When it happens:**

1. **Don't decide immediately.** Give it a week.

2. **Review what you've done.** Listen to your episodes. See your progress.

3. **Lower the bar.** Maybe 100 episodes becomes 50. Maybe 2/week becomes 1/month. Adjust, don't quit.

4. **Remember why you started.** Re-read your goals.

5. **Take a break.** Sometimes you need rest, not quitting.

**You've come too far to stop now.**
