# REWARD SYSTEM
## Celebrate Everything

**ADHD brains need external rewards. Don't wait until episode 100 to celebrate.**

---

## THE PHILOSOPHY

### Why Rewards Matter

**For ADHD:**
- External motivation compensates for dopamine deficiency
- Breaks large goal into achievable chunks
- Makes the journey enjoyable, not just the destination
- Prevents burnout

**Key Principle:** **Frequent small rewards > One big reward at the end**

---

## DAILY MICRO-REWARDS

**After each work session:**

**If it went well:**
- Favorite snack
- 15 min of fun activity
- Check off the box (dopamine hit)

**If it was hard but you did it:**
- EXTRA treats
- Longer break
- Extra praise for yourself

**Even if you just did 5 minutes:**
- Still reward
- Something > nothing
- Maintain positive association

## BACKWARDS REWARDS

### Reward Before Working (Yes, Really)

**Sometimes reward BEFORE task to:**
- Make working more appealing
- Reduce resistance
- Associate work with pleasure

**Examples:**
- Favorite coffee while prepping
- Comfy clothes for recording
- Good snacks during editing session
- Work in favorite location

**Why this works:**
- Makes the task less aversive
- Builds positive associations
- Easier to start

## ANTI-PATTERNS TO AVOID

**DON'T:**
- Wait until episode 100 for first real reward
- Punish yourself for missing days/deadlines
- Skip rewards because "I should be doing this anyway"
- Tie rewards to unrealistic timelines
- Use rewards that make you feel worse (alcohol, junk food binges, etc.)

**DO:**
- Celebrate frequently
- Reward effort, not just results
- Plan rewards in advance
- Make rewards guilt-free
- Choose rewards that energize you
