**THE MOMENT:**
You're standing at 13,000 feet altitude in the Bolivian Andes. Before you: massive stone blocks. Some weigh **130 tons**. They're cut with laser-like precision. Perfect right angles. Interlocking joints (no mortar needed). Drill holes so precise they look machine-made.

This is Puma Punku, part of the ancient Tiwanaku complex. Built ~1,500 years ago by a civilization that supposedly had no writing system, no wheel, no iron tools, and no modern technology.

**And yet: The precision rivals modern machine shops.**

Stone blocks fit together so tightly, you can't slide a credit card between them. Surfaces flat to within fractions of a millimeter. Complex geometric shapes (H-blocks, I-blocks) repeated with identical measurements.

Ancient astronaut theorists look at this and say: **"Impossible. Aliens did it."**

Archaeologists look at this and say: **"Impressive. Humans did it."**

Same stones. Two completely different stories.

## ðŸ“– OVERVIEW

**Puma Punku:**
- Part of Tiwanaku civilization (pre-Incan, ~200-1000 CE)
- High-altitude megalithic site (13,000+ ft above sea level)
- Features massive andesite and diorite blocks (some 130+ tons)
- Precision stonework (flat surfaces, right angles, interlocking joints)

**Believer case:** Precision impossible for ancient technology; megalithic blocks impossible to move/lift; drill holes and cuts suggest power tools; alien technology or guidance

**Skeptic case:** Precision achievable with stone tools + skill + time; blocks moved with ramps, levers, manpower; "drill holes" made with copper tubes + sand abrasive; earthquakes and Spanish looting explain ruined state

**The debate:** Did aliens help build Puma Punku, or did ancient humans not get enough credit?

### WE KNOW HOW THEY DID IT (BECAUSE ARCHAEOLOGISTS TESTED IT)

**Dr. Jean-Pierre Protzen (UC Berkeley):**

- Proved: Precision achievable with Bronze Age tools

### HOW TO CUT DIORITE WITHOUT POWER TOOLS

**The Technique:**

**1. Copper Tubes + Sand Abrasive**

**How Drill Holes Were Made:**
- Hollow copper tube (rotated by hand or bow-drill)
- **Sand (quartz) as abrasive** (harder than diorite)
- Water + sand mixture applied to cutting edge
- Tube rotates â†’ sand grinds through stone

**Protzen's Experiment:**
- Successfully drilled diorite using this method
- **Time:** Slow (hours per hole), but WORKS
- Result: **Identical smooth-walled holes** as Puma Punku

**2. Stone Hammers (Pounding)**

**How Surfaces Were Flattened:**
- **Dolerite hammerstones** (harder than andesite)
- Repeatedly strike surface (pecking technique)
- Gradually wear down high spots
- **Final polish:** Abrasive sand + water

**Protzen's Experiment:**
- Achieved flat surfaces within 1-2 mm accuracy
- **Time:** Weeks of labor per block
- Result: Proves ancient people COULD achieve this precision

**3. Measuring Tools**

**Surveying Without Lasers:**
- **Water levels:** Troughs filled with water (natural level reference)
- **String lines:** Taut strings for straight edges
- **Wooden templates:** Standardized shapes (explains identical H-blocks)

**Skeptic Point:**
"You don't need aliens. You need patience, skill, and LOTS of time."

### HOW TO MOVE 130-TON BLOCKS

**The Method:**

**1. Ramps & Levers**

**Ramp Construction:**
- **Earth/stone ramps** built incrementally
- Blocks dragged up ramps using ropes
- **Demolished after construction** (evidence eroded over 1,500 years)

**2. Rollers & Sledges**

**Wooden Sledges:**
- Blocks placed on sledges
- **Rollers** beneath (logs)
- Lubricant (water, clay) reduces friction

**Manpower:**
- 130-ton block = **~1,300 workers pulling** (100 kg per person)
- Tiwanaku population: 10,000-20,000 (enough labor)

**3. Gradual Transport**

**Not One Trip:**
- **Move in stages:** 100 meters/day
- 10 km journey = ~100 days
- With workforce rotation: **feasible**

**Evidence:**
- Ancient quarries show evidence of ramp systems
- **No mysteryâ€”just hard work**
