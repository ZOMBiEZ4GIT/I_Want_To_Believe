### The Contactee Context

Let's place Van Tassel in his proper context: the 1950s contactee movement.

After Kenneth Arnold's 1947 sighting and the Roswell incident, America went UFO-crazy. And a subset of enthusiasts claimed direct contact with friendly Space Brothers, usually from Venus or other planets.

The contactees shared common features:
- Telepathic or physical contact with human-looking aliens
- Messages of peace, spirituality, and warnings about nuclear weapons
- Invitations to ride in spacecraft
- Missions to spread the aliens' message to humanity

The most famous contactees were:
- **George Adamski** (met Venusians, claimed photos)
- **Truman Bethurum** (contacted by Captain Aura Rhanes from Clarion)
- **Orfeo Angelucci** (mystical contact experiences)

**Not one was ever verified. Not one produced evidence beyond testimony.**

Van Tassel fits this pattern perfectly. The difference is he built something, which gives his claims tangible form. But building something doesn't prove alien contactâ€”it proves construction skills.

- Manipulating cellular "time" doesn't enable time travel

Van Tassel was good at *sounding* scientific without actual rigor.
