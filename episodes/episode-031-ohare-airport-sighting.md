### THE MISIDENTIFICATION THEORIES

**Theory 1: HELICOPTER**

**The Claim:**
- **News helicopter or medical helicopter** passing over airport
- **Witnesses misidentified** (distance, angle)
- Grey color = helicopter body
- Hovering = helicopter capability

**Problems:**
- **No helicopter reported** in area (FAA checked)
- Helicopters are LOUD (no sound reported)
- **Cloud punch:** Helicopters don't do this

**Skeptic Hedge:**
"Probably not helicopter, but possible."

**Theory 2: WEATHER BALLOON**

**The Claim:**
- **Metallic weather balloon**
- Released from nearby location, drifted over airport
- Grey, disc-like when viewed from below

**Problems:**
- **Balloons drift** (this was stationary)
- Balloons don't punch through clouds (ascend slowly)
- **No balloon launch reported** that day

**Theory 3: LENTICULAR CLOUD**

**FAA's Explanation:**
- **Lenticular cloud:** Lens-shaped cloud formation
- Appears metallic/grey
- Can hover (cloud formations stationary relative to ground)

**How It Explains Sighting:**
- **Metallic appearance:** Lenticular clouds can look solid
- **Hovering:** Clouds don't move with wind (formed by air flow over obstacles)
- **Disappearance:** Cloud dissipated

**The Cloud Hole:**
- **Natural phenomenon:** "Hole-punch clouds" (fallstreak holes)
- Occur when supercooled water droplets freeze suddenly
- Creates circular hole in cloud layer

**Skeptic Argument:**
"Witnesses saw lenticular cloud, coincidentally near a natural hole-punch formation."

**Problems With This Theory:**
- **Witnesses describe disc** (solid object, not cloud)
- **Punched through:** Active verb (witnesses saw motion)
- Lenticular clouds don't form quickly over airports (need mountains)

**Theory 4: AIRCRAFT AT DISTANCE**

**The Claim:**
- **Distant aircraft** (commercial or military)
- Angle + distance = appears to hover
- **Contrail or reflection:** Mistaken for object

**Problems:**
- **Pilots would recognize aircraft** (trained observers)
- **15 minutes:** Too long for aircraft to appear stationary
- No contrails reported

### THE "NO PHYSICAL EVIDENCE" PROBLEM

**Skeptic Reality:**
- **No photos:** In 2006 (smartphones not ubiquitous), no one photographed it
- **No video:** No surveillance camera footage (airport cameras pointed at ground/gates, not sky)
- **No radar:** FAA claims no contact

**Skeptic Conclusion:**
"Without physical evidence, it's anecdotal. Witnesses could be mistaken."

### THE GROUPTHINK PROBLEM

**Skeptic Theory:**

**How It Happens:**
1. **First witness sees something** (maybe cloud, aircraft)
2. **Calls it out:** "UFO over the gate!"
3. **Others look, see... something** (cloud, light, aircraft at distance)
4. **Confirmation bias:** First witness's interpretation spreads
5. **Group consensus:** "We all saw a disc"

**Psychological Mechanism:**
- **Social proof:** If others believe, I believe
- **Memory reconstruction:** Story solidifies through retelling
- By time of Tribune interview (2 months later): **Memory = collective narrative**

**Precedent:**
- Multiple mass sightings explained by groupthink
- Witnesses sincere, but collectively wrong

**Skeptic Point:**
"First witness sees cloud, says 'UFO,' everyone else sees UFO. That's not proof. That's psychology."
