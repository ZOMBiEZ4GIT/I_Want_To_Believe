## Overview

Dr. Steven Greer, former emergency room physician, founded the Disclosure Project in the 1990s to advocate for government disclosure of alleged UFO/extraterrestrial information. In 2001, he organized a National Press Club event featuring dozens of military/government witnesses claiming knowledge of UFO encounters, reverse-engineering programs, and cover-ups.

---

## The Disclosure Project Claims

- Government possesses recovered extraterrestrial craft
- Reverse-engineering programs exist
- Free energy technology derived from alien craft is being suppressed
- Witnesses from military/intelligence willing to testify
- Truth embargo maintained for national security/corporate profit reasons

---

## The 2001 Press Conference

Greer presented:

- 20+ witnesses with military/government backgrounds
- Testimony about UFO encounters, recovery operations, cover-ups
- Call for Congressional hearings
- Demand for witness protection and immunity

---

## The Appeal

Greer's message resonates because:

- Offers hope (free energy, peaceful aliens, end to secrecy)
- Provides community and purpose
- Gives voice to whistleblower narratives
- Frames disclosure as moral imperative

---

## The Problems

**Lack of Evidence:**

Despite decades of advocacy:

- No physical evidence produced
- No documents proving reverse-engineering programs
- No free energy devices demonstrated
- Witness testimony remains unverified

**Escalating Claims:**

Greer's claims have become increasingly extraordinary:

- CE-5 protocols (contacting aliens through meditation)
- Channeling extraterrestrial messages
- Claims of assassination attempts
- Alternative medicine and consciousness-based healing
- Charging thousands of dollars for "contact experiences"

**Witness Credibility:**

Many witnesses have:

- No direct knowledge (heard rumors, not firsthand)
- Questionable credentials
- Contradictory accounts
- Financial ties to disclosure movement

---

## My Take

**The Value:**

Greer deserves credit for:

- Reducing stigma around UFO discussion
- Encouraging reporting
- Creating platform for witnesses
- Advocating for transparency

**The Problems:**

- Extraordinary claims without evidence
- Financial exploitation (expensive CE-5 retreats, documentaries)
- Moving from advocacy to cultish movement
- Undermining legitimate UAP research with unsubstantiated claims
- Conspiracy thinking (anyone questioning him is "disinformation")

**The Tragedy:**

Greer could have been effective advocate for serious UFO investigation. Instead, he's become self-promoter making increasingly wild claims, charging followers for "alien contact experiences," and damaging credibility of disclosure advocacy.

**The Bottom Line:**

Want government transparency? Great. But demanding disclosure of programs that don't exist, claiming aliens are here based on meditation experiences, and charging people thousands for "contact" sessions isn't advocacyâ€”it's exploitation.

Greer represents how genuine desire for truth can evolve into belief-driven movement immune to evidence.
