**THE MOMENT:**
October 21, 1978, 7:06 PM. Frederick Valentich, 20-year-old pilot, is flying a Cessna 182 over Bass Strait, Australia. Solo flight, Melbourne to King Island. Clear evening.

### THE MISIDENTIFICATION THEORIES

**Theory 1: FLEW INVERTED, SAW OWN LIGHTS**

**The Claim (Most Popular Skeptic Theory):**
- Valentich experienced **spatial disorientation**
- Flew UPSIDE DOWN (inverted)
- **Saw own landing lights** reflected in calm ocean surface
- Thought reflection was "aircraft below him"

**How This Explains Transmission:**
- "Four bright lights" = Own four landing/navigation lights
- "Orbiting me" = He's turning, lights stay with him (because they're HIS)
- "Green light" = Navigation light (planes have green wingtip light)
- "Long shape" = Distorted reflection

**Spatial Disorientation:**
- Common pilot error (especially inexperienced at night)
- Lose sense of up/down
- **Think you're level when inverted**

**Crash:**
- Eventually realizes error, tries to correct
- **Too late:** Crashes into water
- "Metallic sounds" = Impact with water

**Problems:**

**1. Robey Didn't Notice:**
- Flight controller didn't notice altitude changes

**2. Valentich Described "Orbiting":**
