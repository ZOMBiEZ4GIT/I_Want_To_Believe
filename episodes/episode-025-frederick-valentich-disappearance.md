**Bass Strait History - The "Bass Strait Triangle":**

**Overview:**
- Australia's version of Bermuda Triangle
- Mysterious area from southern coast of Victoria, across Bass Strait, into Tasmania
- Known for unexplained disappearances of ships and aircraft

**Major Disappearances:**

**Maritime:**
- **SS Amelia J (1920):** Vanished without trace shortly after entering Bass Strait
  - Australian military search conducted
  - **Two military search aircraft ALSO vanished** during search (never found)

**Aviation:**
- **Miss Hobart (October 1934):** Airliner vanished in **perfect weather** with 11 people (9 passengers, 2 pilots)
  - **No wreckage or bodies ever found** despite extensive military searches
- **Loina (1935):** Holyman airliner crashed near Flinders Island, 5 lost (3 crew, 2 passengers)
  - **No bodies recovered**
- **Frederick Valentich (1978):** Most famous case

**Environmental Conditions:**
- **Westerly winds** create unpredictable weather patterns
- **Strong currents** sweep debris away quickly
- **Shallow water with tall waves and short swells** (dangerous conditions)
- **Sudden weather changes** (clear to storm in minutes)
- **Under-experienced pilots + extreme conditions** = cause of most crashes

**Modern Assessment:**
- Not supernatural (despite "Triangle" name)
- **Dangerous natural conditions** explain most incidents
- **Search difficulty:** Large area, deep sections, strong currents = wreckage easily lost
