### WE KNOW HOW STONEHENGE WAS BUILT (BECAUSE WE DID IT)

**Experimental Archaeology:**

**Moving Sarsen Stones:**

- **Result:** 100-130 people moved 40 tons successfully

### PRECISION ENGINEERING = PATIENCE, NOT LASERS

**The Lintels:**

**How Curved Joints Were Made:**
- **Stone hammers** (dolerite pounders)
- **Pecking technique:** Repeatedly strike stone, wearing it down
- **Months per lintel:** Time-consuming, but achievable
- **Shaping curves:** Trial and error, measuring with rope/wooden templates

**How Lintels Were Lifted:**

**Experiment (1999, Julian Richards):**
- **Earthen ramp:** Build ramp of soil/timber, drag lintel up
- **Cantilever method:** Stack logs beneath lintel, incrementally raise
- **Result:** Both methods work (no cranes needed)

**Mortise and Tenon:**
- **Not unique to Stonehenge:** Used in woodworking (transferred to stone)
- **Precision:** Achievable with stone tools + patience
