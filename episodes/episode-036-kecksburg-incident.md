**THE MOMENT:**
December 9, 1965. Evening (~4:45 PM EST).

**Multiple witnesses across six states** (Michigan, Ohio, Pennsylvania, West Virginia, Ontario, New York) see:

**A fireball streaking across the sky.** Bright. Blue-white light. Smoke trail. Descending.

**Different from meteor:** Controlled descent. Turns. Slows. Doesn't burn up completely.

**Kecksburg, Pennsylvania** (tiny village, ~1,000 people):

**Witnesses see object crash into woods.** Blue smoke. Impact. Fire reported (minor).

**Local volunteer firefighters respond.** Enter woods. Find:

**An object.** Acorn-shaped. **Size of Volkswagen Beetle.** Metallic. Bronze-gold color.

**Symbols around base.** Hieroglyphic-like. Unrecognizable.

**One witness (firefighter):** "It was smooth, seamless. Warm to touch. Like nothing I'd seen."

"Nothing was found."

**Local newspaper:** **"The object was removed on a flatbed truck. We saw it."**

**60 years later:** Government has NEVER explained what was removed.

**Believers:** Crashed alien craft, Nazi secret weapon, or time capsule. Military cover-up.

**Skeptics:** Meteor, satellite debris, or hoax. Witnesses mistaken/exaggerating.

### THE METEOR EXPLANATION

**Skeptic Position:**
- **Fireball = meteor** (natural phenomenon)
- **Multi-state visibility:** Consistent with large meteor entering atmosphere
- **Crash = meteor fragments** (broke apart, debris scattered)

**Dr. Paul Annear (Astronomer, 1960s):**
"The descriptions match a bolide [bright meteor]. Common, natural."

**Trajectory "Changes":**
- **Skeptic explanation:** Optical illusion (perspective shift as meteor moves)
- **Not actual turns:** Appears to turn from different viewing angles

### NO OBJECT RECOVERED (SKEPTIC VERSION)

**Air Force Statement (Taken at Face Value):**
- **Search conducted:** Military/police checked woods
- **Result:** "No object, no debris"
- **Why search at all?** Public reports (military investigated, found nothing unusual)

**Alternative Witness Interpretation:**

**What Firefighters Saw:**
- **Skeptic theory:** Debris field (meteor fragments, burnt vegetation)
- **Darkness + expectations:** Witnesses expected "crash," saw ambiguous shapes, filled in details
- **"Acorn shape":** Misidentified natural formation or debris clump

**Why Different Descriptions?**
- **Skeptic point:** If real craft, all witnesses should agree on details
- **Reality:** Descriptions vary (size, exact shape, symbol details)
- **Suggests:** Witnesses saw different things or imagined details

### THE "HIEROGLYPHICS" PROBLEM

**Skeptic Challenges:**
